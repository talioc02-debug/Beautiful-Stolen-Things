Story 1 — The Stolen Beach

Listen and read
Story 1 — Natural voice
Click play to listen
🔊 Needs internet connection for best voice quality

Jamaica is a beautiful island in the Caribbean Sea.

In 2008, something very unusual happened there.

There was a beach in Jamaica. It was 400 metres long. It had white sand and clear blue water.

One morning, people arrived at the beach. But the beach was not there! The sand was gone. All of it.

Where did the sand go?

The police investigated. They think that thieves used about 500 trucks. The trucks came at night. They took all the sand.

The sand was very valuable. It cost about one million dollars!

Nobody saw anything. Nobody heard anything.

No one was arrested. The case is still a mystery today.

Grammar pattern — There WAS / There WERE
There was a beach. (= one beach)
There were 500 trucks. (= many trucks)
There was no arrest. (= zero arrests)

Story 2 — Two Famous Paintings

Listen and read
Story 2 — Natural voice
Click play to listen

Leonardo da Vinci was a great artist from Italy. He lived from 1452 to 1519.

He painted two very famous paintings.

Painting 1: The Mona Lisa

She is a woman with long dark hair. She has a very famous smile. Nobody knows why she is smiling!

The Mona Lisa is in Paris, France, in a museum called the Louvre.

In 1911, a man stole the Mona Lisa! His name was Vincenzo Peruggia. He was Italian.

He hid the painting in his apartment for two years. Then the police found him and arrested him.

The painting went back to the Louvre. Now millions of people visit it every year.

Painting 2: Lady with an Ermine

A young woman holds a small white animal. The animal is called an ermine.

This painting is in Poland, in a city called Krakow.

During World War Two, the Nazis took the painting. But after the war, Poland got the painting back.

Today, both paintings are very famous and very valuable.

Grammar pattern — Simple Past (regular and irregular)
He lived in Italy. (live + d = regular)
He stole the painting. (steal becomes stole = irregular)
The police found him. (find becomes found = irregular)
Poland got it back. (get becomes got = irregular)

Useful in Daily Life

These phrases come from the stories. You can use them every day!

That is very unusual!
È molto insolito!
Use it when something is strange.
"You eat pizza for breakfast? That is very unusual!"
It is still a mystery.
È ancora un mistero.
Use it when there is no explanation.
"We lost the keys. It is still a mystery!"
Nobody saw anything.
Nessuno ha visto niente.
Use it when no one noticed something.
"The cake disappeared. Nobody saw anything!"
It is worth a lot of money.
Vale molto.
Use it for valuable things.
"This old painting is worth a lot of money."
Where did it go?
Dove è andato?
Use it when something disappears.
"My coffee was here. Where did it go?"
Nobody knows why.
Nessuno sa perché.
Use it when there is no reason.
"She is smiling. Nobody knows why!"
He got it back.
Lo ha recuperato.
Use it when someone recovers something.
"I lost my phone but I got it back."
It happened at night.
È successo di notte.
Use it to say when something happened.
"The party was very loud. It happened at night."
